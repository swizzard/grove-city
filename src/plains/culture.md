# culture
* think boughs folk are snobby and hills folk are too anxious
## 'e-nniwak
* not (really/officially) gendered
* values
    * independence
    * self-reliance
    * self-assuredness
    * having, declaring, and working to satisfy desires
    * cleverness/inventiveness
    * problem-solving
* barter
    * 'getting a good deal' is nniwak
    * not historically too important b/c clans mostly migrated together so minimal resource disparities
    * humorous city folk/tavern song about a girl who red paperclips her way from a single kkamonn stalk to someone else cooking a meal for her and the guy she's courting
    * "i only have my love and this _____"

## godparent/older cousin/mentor culture
* nnonna (mentor) — na'o (mentee)
* twins get same nnonna, or siblings/partners
* someone who's at the birth, teaches life skills, has "the talk," helps kid prepare for adulthood ritual, gives dating advice, etc.
    * traditionally, they would buy the kid a saddle, riding outfit, &c and accompany them on their first ride
    * nowadays it's a gift of formalwear (kkippiko ttewa but also 'church'/'interview' clothes) and some money, and either carrying the kid on their shoulders (ideally while wearing fake oryx horns) or (mostly) pulling a wheeled replica oryx on which the celebrant rides
* some young plains folk have more than one person in this role. this is still pretty uncommon and can happen if the original mentor is older or unwell, or the mentee's interests or aptitudes differ strongly from those of the original mentor and another community member would be a better resource
* **NOT SEXUAL**
    * not traditionally or historically either
## music
* smaller/portable string instruments
* drums
* singing
    * taunting inter-generational chants/rhymes growing up
    * everyone can at least kind of sing, or at least everyone pretends everyone can at least kind of sing
        * ~50s teen movie vibes: impromptu singing to woo/cajole/motivate/insult expected and not embarrassing
    * finding someone you can do (close) harmony with is exciting and special
* "musician" isn't a job because/but a lot of people jam with friends or compose & perform music for events or celebrations
* doo-wop groups/barbershop quartets
## housing
* mostly live in apartment blocks in the Low Quarter
* 4-5 stories
* rooftop gardens with chickens
* balconies
* some religious rites need open sky

* livestock is Important
    * traditionally _kkippi_ and _'owi_ but in city just _'owi_
        * _kkippi_ = (semi-)domesticated [oryx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scimitar_oryx)
            * breeding your own never as valorized or common as capturing & taming wild ones
        * _'owi_ = [bobwhite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_bobwhite) 
            * ~9-12" tall, 1.5-2lbs
            * often sit down under stress
            * each family has their own, usually kept on the roof
                * communal coops but everybody knows whose is whose
* rooftop/balcony gardens
    * ~ritual homesteading
        * not necessarily expectation of self-sufficiency but everyone thinks you're kind of a fuckup if you aren't at least growing a few flowers or keeping a healthy _'owi_
    * traditional plants hearty/low-maintenance
    * roofs of commercial buildings used commercially
        * mostly _'i'i_, some _'a'i'_
            * _ttinta_ mostly grown out of town
            * _kkamonn_ is very easy/cheap to grow and nobody uses enough of it to make it worth growing commercially

## food
* methods
    * open fire
    * spit-roasting
    * shallow, open clay cooking vessels
        * more like a paella pan than a wok
        * placed under whatever's roasting to catch any rendered fat
    * burying stuff in coals
        * inner layer of woven reeds/grasses, outer layer of mud/clay
* preserved/fermented stuff buried in the ground for you to come back to next year
* seasonal/local (nomadic)
        * traditionally you e.g. ate wild rice at the time of year when you harvest the wild rice at the place that grows wild rice
* _'owi_
* _nnaw_ (schmaltz)
* _wwitteko nnaw_ (honey)
* _ggeni_ (egg)
* _ttinta_ (wild rice)
* _'a'i_ (yams/sweet potatoes)
    * _'a'iko 'eto_ ("sweet potato jam") — mashed sweet potatoes (+ honey, maybe spices) slow-cooked
* _kkamonn_ (wild onion)
* _mmi'i_ (chiles)

##  employment/livelihood
* small-scale community farming on apartment roofs
    * _'owi_, _'a'i_, _kkamonn_
* stevadoring
* grocers, food service
* "unskilled" labor for hillsfolk
    * janitorial, carrying stuff, manual labor, etc.

## sports
* polo/rugby/capture-the-flag
    * evolved from livestock raids
        * _'owi_ under the arm etc
    * rugby except the ball is weighted and feathered like a shuttlecock
        * hard to kick/throw accurately for distance
* wrestling
