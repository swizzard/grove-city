# vocab
## verbs
wep
  : (vi) - be alert, look out, keep watch
  : (vt) - see, look at, look for

mmattam
  : (vi) - be/stay silent, not speak
  : (vi) - refrain from doing/refuse to do

'ettin
  : (vt) - bury, store, put up    
        <i class="fa-solid fa-book"></i> food &c, not bodies    
        <i class="fa-solid fa-book"></i> also used for e.g. roasting among coals

nnattin
  : (vt) - dig up, unwrap, open

'emmamm
  : (vt) - crush, grind, mash

nnen
  : (vt) - have/make (someone else do something),
    get (something from someone or someone to do something)        
        <i class="fa-solid fa-book"></i> not by force    
        <i class="fa-solid fa-book"></i> possibly trickery but also just paying for something

pemmam
  : (vt) - carry, bear
  : (vi) -  worry about, + _tti_

ttewatti
  : literally "in (the) uniform"

     <i class="fa-solid fa-book"></i> appropriately-dressed, convincingly-dressed, well-dressed, handsome/beautiful        
    * kids in church clothes (_kkeppiko ttewa_, etc)
    * dressed up for a date
        * complement coming from your bros, probably judgmental coming from your family, an insult coming from your date themselves
    * firefighter/sports/parade/military uniforms
    * fair, reasonable (of a price or terms)
    * neat, well-maintained (of a place)
    * resembling, imitating, "dressed as," pretending to be (thing being imitated + _-ko_)
        * insulting — unoriginality/copying is stigmatized
        * `we'akow ke'ako ttewatti` - "the ['akow](#akow) is dressed as a person" →"'akow pretend to be people" →you can't trust some people
        * `ke'ako ttewatti 'akow` - "'akow dressed as a person," thief, 'biter'
